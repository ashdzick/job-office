# Avoid Patterns

## AI speak — never use these
<!-- These are the patterns that make writing sound generated rather than human. -->
<!-- Example: "leverage" / "it's worth noting that" / "I want to be clear that" / "at the end of the day" -->

- "leverage" (use "use" instead)
- "it's worth noting"
- "in today's fast-paced world"
- "I wanted to reach out"
- "circle back"
- "touch base"
- "move the needle" (unless used deliberately)
- "utilize" (use "use")
- "ensure" when "make sure" is clearer
- Sentences that start with "Certainly," or "Absolutely,"

## Filler openers — never start with these
<!-- Example: "I am excited to apply..." / "As a [job title], I have..." / "I have always been passionate about..." -->

- "I'm excited to..."
- "I'd love the opportunity to..."
- "As a [job title], I..."
- "I have always been passionate about..."
- "Thank you for your consideration"

## Vague claims — always back with evidence
<!-- These phrases are fine if they're followed by proof. Avoid them when they're standing alone. -->
<!-- Example: "strong communicator" / "detail-oriented" / "results-driven" -->

- Any trait claim without evidence ("strong communicator", "collaborative", "detail-oriented", "strategic thinker")
- "I work well under pressure"
- "I'm a quick learner"
