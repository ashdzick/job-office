# Cover Letter Rules

<!-- These layer on top of your shared voice rules. Use this file for anything specific to cover letters — structure, openers, closers, what to include or skip. -->

## Structure
<!-- How many paragraphs? What does each one do? -->
<!-- Example: 3 to 4 paragraphs. Open with something specific to the role. Make the case in the middle. Close with confidence, not a plea. -->

3 to 4 paragraphs. No more. Open with something specific to the role or company — not an intro about myself. Close briefly and confidently.

## Greeting and sign-off
<!-- Do you use "Dear Hiring Manager"? Do you sign off? -->
<!-- Example: No greeting line. No sign-off. Just the letter body. The format makes clear who it's from. -->

No greeting line. No sign-off. Body only.

## First sentence rule
<!-- How do you open? What are you never allowed to start with? -->
<!-- Example: Never start with "I". The first sentence should give the reader something — context, a specific detail, a frame — before introducing me. -->

Never open with "I". Lead with something that signals I've read the role and have relevant context.

## Tone for cover letters specifically
<!-- Does your cover letter tone differ from your general writing? More formal? Less? -->
<!-- Example: Slightly more considered than my LinkedIn writing. But still direct — contractions are fine, I'm not writing a legal brief. -->

Professional but human. Contractions are fine. It should sound like me in a senior meeting — not me on LinkedIn, not me in a legal document.

## Gaps
<!-- How do you want to handle qualification gaps? Ignore them? Address head-on? -->
<!-- Example: Address the biggest gap once, briefly, then pivot to what's transferable. Never pretend a gap doesn't exist — it reads as either oblivious or dishonest. -->

Address the main gap directly and briefly. Pivot to what's transferable. Don't dwell.
